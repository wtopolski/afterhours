Dark Detector

A USB-C powered light-dependent LED driver. The circuit senses ambient light through a photoresistor and activates an LED in darkness — a minimal analog night-light.

Overview

How it works

A photoresistor and a 10kΩ resistor form a voltage divider that controls the base of a BC546B NPN transistor. In darkness the LDR resistance rises, forward-biasing the transistor, which switches on the LED through a 220Ω current-limiting resistor.

Bright

LDR resistance is low. Voltage divider pulls the base below 0.7V threshold. Transistor is cut off.

LED → OFF

Dark

LDR resistance is high. Base voltage rises above 0.7V. Transistor saturates and current flows through the LED.

LED → ON

Circuit design

Designed in EasyEDA. The circuit uses only VBUS and GND from the USB-C breakout — no data lines required.

V_R2 = 5.0V − 2.0V − 0.2V = 2.8V
I_LED = 2.8V / 220Ω = 12.7 mA

I_B = (1.2V − 0.7V) / 10kΩ = 0.05 mA
I_C = β × I_B = 200 × 0.05 = 10 mA
